Zion National Park
Utah's red cliffs and winding canyons

Zion National Park was the second stop on my Spring Break 2025 Utah trip, right after Bryce Canyon. Zion’s massive red cliffs, narrow slot canyons, jagged rocks amidst the fog, and bright blue skies made it one of the most incredible places I’ve ever hiked. I could easily see why it’s one of the most visited national parks in the U.S.

View of Angels Landing Zion’s towering red cliffs The Narrows

Quick Facts
Category Information
Location Southwestern Utah
Year Established 1919
Famous Feature Steep red cliffs and slot canyons
Best Time to Visit Spring or Fall (avoid summer heat)
Fun Fact The Virgin River carved the entire main canyon over millions of years

Top Viewpoints & Trails
  1. Angels Landing
  2. The Narrows
  3. Canyon Overlook Trail

Personal note: I enjoyed every single hike I went on those two days I spent in the park. But I will say that hiking through the water in the narrows was unbelievable. The water was really cold but it was so cool seeing the amazing rock structures formed by thousands of years of erosion from the Virgin River.
