Alpine lakes, glaciers, and rugged mountain scenery

Even though I only got to see a few areas near the water and do small hikes because of heavy snow that winter, the dramatic peaks and icy lakes left a huge impression. Quick Facts
Category Information
Location Northern Montana
Year Established 1910
Famous Feature Glaciers, Going-to-the-Sun Road, alpine lakes
Best Time to Visit Late spring through early fall (roads and trails open)
Fun Fact Glacier once had over 100 glaciers; many have since shrunk significantly

Top Spots & Trails
  1. Going-to-the-Sun Road (scenic drive)
  2. Lake McDonald
  3. Grinnell Glacier viewpoint

Personal note: Because I visited in winter, most of my time was spent near the lakes and on shorter trails due to snow coverage, but even those limited views convinced me how special national parks can be. I can't wait to come back in a warmer season and hike farther into the backcountry.
